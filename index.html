Philosophy

What remains.

A wedding lasts a day. The photographs stay much longer. Years from now, the details may fade, but the people, relationships, and moments shared together often become even more meaningful.

My approach is simple: to create an honest record of the day as it happened. Not just how it looked, but who was there, how they laughed, and what it felt like to be together.

The Photographer

Pratyush Dadhich

I am a photographer and designer based in Italy. I believe photographs become more meaningful with time. Long after a wedding day has passed, they remain as a record of the people who were there, the relationships they shared, and the moments that brought them together.

My work is focused on preserving those moments honestly. I photograph weddings with a documentary approach, paying attention not only to the major events of the day, but also to the quieter interactions in between. The result is a collection of images that feels less like a photoshoot and more like a memory.
